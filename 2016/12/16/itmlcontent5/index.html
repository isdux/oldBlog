Supervised Learning

We discuss supervised learning starting from the simplest case, which is learning a class from its positive and negative examples. We generalize and discuss the case of multiple classes, then regression, where the outputs are continuous.

Learning a Class from Examples

Note that if $x_1$ and $x_2$ are real-valued, there are infinitely many such h for which this is satisfied, namely, for which the error, E, is 0, but given a future example somewhere close to the boundary between positive and negative examples, different candidate hypotheses may make different predictions.

注意到如果 $x_1$ 和 $x_2$ 是实数值，这里就会有无限多个让人满意的 h(x)，也就是说，h 的错误估计，E(h | X) == 0, 但是对于将来的样本有时候会接近这个 h 中的错误与正确的样本边界线，不同的候选假设可能产生不同的预测。

This is the problem of generalization—that is, how well our hypothesis will correctly classify future examples that are not part of the training set.

这可以概括为一个问题，一个多么好的假设将让我们可以对不属于训练集合一部分的未来数据正确的分类。

Actually, depending on X and H , there may be several $S{i}$ and $G{j}$ which
respectively make up the S-set and the G-set.

Every member of the S-set is consistent with all the instances, and there are no consistent hypotheses that are more specific. Similarly, every member of the G-set is consistent with all the instances, and there are no consistent hypotheses that are more general.

每一个S集合的成员对于所有的实例都是一致的，但却有一个非常特殊的不同的预测(S-范围最小)。同样的，每一个G集合的成员对于所有的实例都是一致的，但却有一个非常普通的不同的预测(G-范围最大)。

These two make up the boundary sets and any hypothesis between them is consistent and is part of the version space.

这两个特殊的存在产生了一个边界集合，其他任何的预测都是在这个范围之内，这两个边界之间的部分(边界集合)就是版本空间。
